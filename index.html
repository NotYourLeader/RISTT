Secondary V3 with CP: key points, evidence and assumptions

This section summarises the main planning decisions, constraints and outstanding items behind the Secondary V3 timetable. Each key-point group includes a visual evidence block: either a live timetable snapshot drawn from the embedded data, or a clearly labelled planning diagram where the decision is not directly encoded in aSc.

Maths structure

  • Grade 6 Maths has been blocked with three classes running concurrently, allowing for two extended classes and four standard classes.
  • Grade 10 Maths will be streamable, although not all Maths lessons happen concurrently.
  • In Grade 10, Maths has been blocked against English to support the required grouping structure.

DP2 Maths changes

  • Two DP2 Maths AI HL classes have been created, reducing the original AI HL class of 21 into two smaller groups.
  • DP2 AA SL, which had one student, has been collapsed.
  • That student has been added to DP2 AA HL, and the class has been renamed DP2 AA MX.

Staff allocation and load controls

  • Allocations have been updated in consideration of recent POR allocations.
  • LTL SE allocations have been limited to 15 periods.
  • LTLC allocations have been limited to 16 periods.
  • Every effort has been made to reduce the number of preps and the spread of grade levels per teacher.
  • Where possible, teachers have been assigned primarily to Grades 6–8 or Grades 9–10.
  • Prior experience and preferences have been taken into account as far as possible.

DP1 subject demand and block movement

  • DP1 Biology now requires three classes, rather than two as indicated by the first round of DP choices.
  • DP1 Global Politics now requires two classes, rather than one as indicated by the first round of DP choices.
  • To address this, one Biology class has been moved into the Group 3 block.
  • Global Politics has been moved into the Group 4 block.
  • This preserves the option for students to take three sciences.

Languages and MOE Arabic

  • Updated language choices have reduced the number of French B classes.
  • This should reduce pressure on the MEL team.
  • A number of Arabic A students have not chosen DP Arabic.
  • Those students will need to take MOE Arabic during lunch or after school because there is no seventh block in the timetable.

MYP, ConnectEd and ToK

  • MYP PHE will take place as single periods, not doubles.
  • Grade 10 has a Week B slot for Moral Education or MYP Core / Personal Project, in addition to ConnectEd.
  • ConnectEd will take place each week for Grades 6–10 on Friday Period 1 for 30 minutes and Monday Period 4 for one hour.
  • ToK will also have 30 minutes on Friday morning and a further one-hour slot during the week.

V3 inclusion checks and still-provisional items

  • The CP/Careers Programme timetable is included in this V3 build through the Careers Program 1 class and CP-related subjects.
  • No classes are allocated double periods, including DP and PHE.
  • ToK teachers have not yet been assigned because the full candidate list is still awaited.
  • Department meetings are still being added. At present, only Maths, English and Science have been allocated a one-hour department meeting.
Note: the current department meeting allocation is a sequencing issue, not an indication of department prioritisation.
